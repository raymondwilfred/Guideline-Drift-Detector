# guideline-drift-detector
An open-source system that automatically detects and explains changes in medical guidelines across versions to prevent outdated clinical practice.

Open Medical Guideline Diff & Drift Detector is an open-source public health intelligence tool designed to automatically track, compare, and explain changes in clinical guidelines released by organizations such as the World Health Organization, National Institute for Health and Care Excellence, and Indian Council of Medical Research.

Medical recommendations frequently evolve, but many hospitals and practitioners continue using outdated PDF documents due to the lack of transparent change-tracking tools. Our system addresses this gap by providing automated version comparison and plain-language explanations of clinically meaningful updates.

 Key Features

Automated Guideline Ingestion
Parses and processes multiple versions of medical guideline documents.

Semantic Drift Detection
Identifies meaningful clinical changes such as threshold updates, dosage modifications, and screening criteria revisions.

Plain-Language Explanations
Converts complex medical edits into easy-to-understand summaries for rapid clinical awareness.

Risk-Based Change Tagging
Classifies updates into Critical, Moderate, and Minor impact levels to help prioritize review.

Open-Source & Extensible
Designed as a FOSS platform to support transparency, community validation, and public health research.

Impact

This project aims to reduce the risk of outdated clinical practice by making guideline evolution transparent, searchable, and explainable. It supports clinicians, researchers, and healthcare institutions in staying aligned with the latest evidence-based recommendations.

 Disclaimer

This tool does not provide medical advice or clinical decision support. It is intended solely for guideline change awareness and research purposes.
